The Games

Escape the Dark Castle...

...is a 'choose-your-own-adventure' game with a twist; you play as a group who either win together or die together!

You each choose a character with traits in specific skills, who have all been imprisoned in the bowels of a creepy castle infested with monsters, traps, and the occasional treasure chest. All you must do is get out alive...

Why have we chosen this game?

  • It's approachable, designed with non-gamers in mind, and runs like a classic fantasy quest for up to 5 players.
  • The game is easy to set up and can be adjusted for a longer or shorter game, although the standard setup only takes around 30 mins to play.
  • The game has a focus on atmosphere, and group strategy.
  • The playthrough is different every time, adding to the tension and suspense as you reveal each stylishly designed card with a new encounter to defeat.
  • As the cards are revealed one-by-one, the game takes on the form of a shared storybook experience.
  • The cards that are chosen can sometimes affect the character of the player who is reading it, meaning that everyone has to take a turn at some point.
  • There is the possibility of adding to the game through expansions that deepen the adventure, from new locations within the castle to gruesome foes with devious abilities.

Betrayal at House on the Hill...

... you and your fellow players explore a supposedly haunted mansion, until one of you turns on the rest. The heroes must then defeat the traitor and escape before it's too late!

Players choose a character to play, each of whom has different trait values in Knowledge, Sanity, Speed, and Strength. As you explore the house its secrets will be revealed until the 'haunt' scenario begins. It is then up to you to do what it takes to escape the House on the Hill and win the game.

Why have we chosen this game?

  • For an in-depth game, the setup is relatively quick and straightforward.
  • Betrayal is more visual and more immersive than Escape the Dark Castle as players can see both the house being explored in front of them and their characters moving around inside it.
  • As players choose how and which parts of the house to explore, the layout of the building will be different every time.
  • The impacts of cards drawn in the game - Omens, Events, and Items - can often be felt across the house, keeping players engaged even when it's not their turn.
  • The heroes can either choose to team up or play as individuals once the 'haunt' begins, winning together or leaving one of their number at the mercy of the evils of the House on the Hill.

Zombies!!!...

...is set in a post-apocalyptic city overrun with zombies, blocking the streets, hiding in the shops, and, most worryingly, congregating on the helipad where there is a helicopter with one spare seat waiting for someone to fight their way through the undead hordes and escape.

You can play either as an individual or make (and break!) alliances with other players as you search for the helicopter pilot before making a break for the helipad. But keep a close eye on your bullets and your health or you might have to start all over again! Cards can be used to give yourself more ammo or health, or to greatly inconvenience other players who have their own eye on that seat in the helicopter...

Why have we chosen this game?

  • Simple rules make it an easy game to get into...
  • ...but card use and the potential for alliances can make for a very strategic game!
  • Character and zombie figurines, and a birds-eye map of the city, make for a realistic gaming experience.
  • Tension increases throughout the game as players get closer to - and sometimes further away from - victory!
  • An ideal game for beginners to tabletop gaming.

Escape the Dark Sector...

...is the second game in the 'Escape' series by Themeborne, this time with a Sci-Fi twist - you play as the beleaguered crew of an impounded starship, who have been imprisoned in the detention block of a vast space station. Using advanced gear and weaponry, you embark on a desperate mission together to find your ship and blast your way home.

This time around, you're overcoming dangers, traps and terrors with a vast arsenal of melee and ranged weaponry to help you combat cyborg guards, alien organisms, and defeat the final boss to get your ship back.

Why have we chosen this game?

  • As with Dark Castle, Dark Sector is approachable, designed with non-gamers in mind and can support up to 5 players
  • Immersive chapter cards take on the form of a shared storybook experience, that changes with every play-through
  • The game has a focus on atmosphere, and group strategy.
  • The cards that are chosen can sometimes affect the character of the player who is reading it, meaning that everyone has to take a turn at some point.
  • There is the possibility of adding to the game through expansions that deepen the adventure, from new locations to alien foes with an array of abilities and weaponry.
